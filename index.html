About

I'm a data scientist based in London, currently completing an MSc in Social Data Science at the London School of Economics.

What I enjoy most about data science is its versatility. I've had the opportunity to apply it across a range of disciplines, including developing a two-stage flight delay prediction system for the airline industry, building a pipeline that automated sales tax calculations for a multimillion-dollar company, and using natural language processing to create a tourism policy uncertainty index for economic research. I enjoy learning new domains and using data to solve problems that have practical, real-world impact.

Before moving into data science, I studied psychology and worked in quantitative research, where I co-authored academic publications and presented research at national and international conferences. Having a background in both the social and data sciences has shaped the way I approach problems today. It encourages me to combine technical rigor with an understanding of human behaviour, challenge assumptions, and approach problems from multiple perspectives. I care not only about building models that perform well, but also about understanding what their results mean and how they can be used to make better decisions.
